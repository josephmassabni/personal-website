Hello, I'm Joseph Massabni! Let me introduce myself.




I am a self-taught programmer and high school student residing in Wisconsin. My adventure into programming began while experimenting with a drag-and-drop application called GameSalad in 7th grade. Eventually, I began looking deeper and deeper into the basics of coding, eventually deciding to take two online Northwestern-sponsored classes in C#/C++ and Java. Ever since learning the basics, I have been experimenting with different technologies solely out of personal interest. From attempting to create games using Unity in my Freshman year of high school to being the head programmer of my school's robotics team to redesigning a website for a local nonprofit, my experiences with programming have been more than just improving my coding skills: they have helped me improve as a student and person.


The best part of programming is its ability to seemingly create something out of nothing. To me, it is amazing that we are able to create something that can affect millions of people just with an idea and a computer. I am especially interested in the link between the virtual world and reality: how the two influence and alter each other. I want to play my part in shaping the digital world through my own personal projects and possibly in even larger ways in the future!


In my free time, I enjoy playing tennis, running, reading about the stock market, and learning about economics. I especially enjoy doing things that require constant improvement and opportunities to improve. But, I would definitely be leaving something out if I didn't mention that I also enjoy contributing on Reddit to procrastinate doing other more productive things!
